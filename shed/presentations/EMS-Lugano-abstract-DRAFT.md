# EMS Lugano Abstract — Multi-model Ensemble Systematic Review

## Current Version (as drafted)

Ensemble forecasts combine predictions from multiple models, reliably improving predictive accuracy compared to component models.
Long established in weather and financial forecasting, ensemble modelling is increasingly used to inform public health intelligence.
Yet a lack of standardised evaluation has made it difficult to translate modelling work during each outbreak into better prepared modelling in practice.
This talk presents three approaches to evaluating multi-model collaborations informing public health policy.
We first present a case study of the European COVID-19 Forecast Hub, where over 50 independent forecasters predicted COVID-19 incidence across Europe over two years.
We found the ensemble outperformed component models in 84% of case forecasts (N=862) and 92% of death forecasts (N=746) at a 1-4 week horizon.
We then consider factors influencing the predictive performance of both the ensemble and its component models.
Specifically, we investigate whether a models' mechanistic or statistical structure affects its ability to forecast across epidemic dynamics, while accounting for multi-layered and non-linear effects on predictive difficulty.
Last, we contextualise the use of multi-model collaborations with a systematic evaluation of ensemble performance across more than a decade of outbreak forecasting, covering influenza, dengue, and other pathogens.
We suggest not only the benefits but also the costs of such collaborative work for public health decision support.
We conclude with the importance of matching multi-model comparison with robust evaluation to improve both epidemic preparedness and real-time response.
