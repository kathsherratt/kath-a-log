# Feedback on detailed research plan (v3)

## Overall

The rationale for focusing on longstanding eating disorders (LSED) is well-articulated



The existing evidence section contains all the right content, but each paragraph currently mixes several themes (prevalence, definitions, treatment, outcomes). I think the section would land more forcefully if each paragraph had a single clear focus, flowing from the scale of the problem through to the gaps your work addresses. I have suggested some rearrangements in the document to show what I mean, though the substance is already all there. Similarly, I have suggested merging Studies 1 and 2 (and Studies 3 and 4) within each work package, as splitting them into separate sub-studies may dilute rather than clarify the narrative.

## Research questions

The two research questions are broad. The first ("How are longstanding eating disorders conceptualised and experienced...?") could sustain a research programme rather than a single PhD. It would strengthen the proposal to sharpen these, perhaps by indicating which aspects of conceptualisation each work package will address, and what would be out of scope. A reader should be able to see the direct line from each question to a specific study and its expected output.

## Feasibility and risk

Could add a brief paragraph acknowledging key risks and possible mitigations (e.g. alternative recruitment routes, a pre-agreed threshold for Delphi consensus).

## Methods for work package 3

Study 5 mentions a target trial emulation framework using EDCRN data, but does not specify which outcome variables, what comparisons will be made, or what statistical approach will be used. A reviewer is likely to ask whether the candidate has the quantitative skills to deliver this, and more detail here would provide reassurance. Study 6 (co-designed care pathways using nominal group technique) would benefit from a clearer account of what the workshops will produce and how recommendations translate into testable outputs.

## Minor points

I have flagged a few places where terms are used without definition (e.g. the IAPT acronym, "broad and fluid" classifications), and where the population and sampling strategy section could be repositioned earlier to provide context before the individual studies. These are detailed in the in-document comments.
