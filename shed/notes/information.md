# information

notes from reading wikipedia

etymology
- objective (etymology: in formation): as a universal force shaping the individual; an autonomous quantity
- subjective (later sense): as sensation, subjective perception; information to an observer

arguments for subjective position [1]
- an object has different information for different people in different situatins
- no one mapping is the "true" mapping
- information can be represented differently for different domains of knowledge
- uncertainty over what is informative
- informative within a domain based on consensus and relevant

[1] Hjørland, B. (2007). Information: Objective or Subjective/Situational?.
